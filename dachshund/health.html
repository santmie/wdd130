Taking Care of Dachshund's Health

Exercise

Dachshunds, like all dogs, require regular exercise to maintain their physical and mental well-being. Despite their small size, Dachshunds have a moderate to high energy level and benefit from daily exercise to burn off excess energy and stay healthy. Two moderately long walks per day should be adequate. Regular walks help keep their muscles toned and promote cardiovascular health.

Dachshunds also enjoy interactive play sessions with their owners. Engage them in games like fetch, tug-of-war, or hide-and-seek to stimulate their minds and keep them active. Puzzle toys and treat-dispensing toys can also provide mental stimulation.

It's worth noting that Dachshunds have a long spine and short legs, which can make them prone to back problems. It's important to avoid activities that involve excessive jumping or putting strain on their backs, such as jumping off furniture or participating in high-impact activities.

Nutrition and Feeding

Maintaining a healthy weight is crucial for Dachshunds to prevent strain on their long backs and minimize the risk of slipped or ruptured discs. Obesity can exacerbate the already existing risk that Dachshunds have due to their unique body structure.

To ensure your Dachshund maintains a healthy weight, it's important to adhere to the recommended portion sizes provided by the manufacturer of your chosen high-quality dog food. Avoid overfeeding and resist the temptation to give in to pleading eyes or offer excessive table scraps. High-fat foods and cooked bones should be avoided, as they can lead to digestive issues or pose a choking hazard.

Since each Dachshund is unique, their nutritional needs may vary. Thus, it is always a good idea to consult with your veterinarian to determine the specific dietary requirements for your Dachshund and to address any individual health concerns or dietary restrictions they may have.

Grooming

Regular brushing, preferably daily or at least a few times a week, is necessary to prevent matting and tangling for Dachshund coat types. Regular baths using a mild dog shampoo are also important to keep their coat clean and healthy. Trimming your Dachshund's nails regularly keeps their nails at a proper length.

Dachshunds are also prone to dental issues, so it's essential to establish a regular dental care routine. Brush your Dachshund's teeth regularly with a dog-specific toothbrush and toothpaste. Dental chews and toys designed to promote dental health can also help keep their teeth clean. Also, check your Dachshund's ears regularly for signs of infection, redness, or excessive wax buildup.

3 Most Common Dachshund Illnesses and How to Treat Them

• Back Disease

Compared to other dog breeds, dachshunds are more prone to intervertebral disc disease (IVDD). IVDD develops when the discs between the spine's vertebrae wear down, potentially compressing the spinal cord and resulting in discomfort, weakness, and even paralysis, especially in the back.

To help prevent or control IVDD in Dachshunds, a number of actions can be taken. One is weight management. In order to lessen the strain on their spine, it's essential to keep your Dachshund at a healthy weight. Weight gain can accelerate intervertebral disc deterioration. Regular exercise is also necessary for maintaining muscular tone and overall health, but it's critical to stay away from activities that place too much stress on your Dachshund's back. Supplements for joints are another option that could provide support for your Dachshund's spine.

While the above steps can help reduce the risk or severity of IVDD in Dachshunds, the condition may still occur despite preventive measures. So it's important to seek veterinary care promptly for proper diagnosis and treatment options for any signs of back or spinal issues.

• Obesity

Obesity is a common health problem in the Dachshund breed. It led to various health problems in Dachshunds, including diabetes, heart disease, joint issues, breathing difficulties, and decreased overall quality of life. It puts extra strain on their joints and spine, increasing the risk of intervertebral disc disease (IVDD), which is a common condition in the breed.

This problem is typically the result of a combination of factors, including overfeeding, a high-fat diet, a lack of exercise, genetic predisposition, and neutering or spaying. Dachshunds have a tendency to overeat, and their owners may unknowingly contribute to this behavior by providing excessive treats or inappropriate portion sizes.

To prevent or manage obesity in Dachshunds, it's crucial to establish a healthy lifestyle. This includes feeding a well-balanced diet with controlled portions, avoiding excessive treats, and incorporating regular exercise. Consult with your veterinarian to determine the appropriate diet and portion sizes for your Dachshund's specific needs.

• Skin Issues

A bacterial or fungal skin infection in a dachshund can result in redness, itching, hair loss, and an unpleasant odor. A veterinarian will often prescribe topical or oral medications, such as antibiotics or antifungal drugs, for treatment. Important preventive actions include keeping up with proper hygiene, frequent grooming, and keeping the dog's living space clean.

Like other dog breeds, dachshunds are prone to parasites like fleas, ticks, and mites, which can itch, irritate, and cause other symptoms in the skin. Controlling parasites effectively is essential. For advice on the best flea and tick prevention products or treatments for your Dachshund, speak with a veterinarian.
