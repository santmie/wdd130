The traits of Smooth, Long, and Wire-Haired Dachshunds can vary slightly depending on their coat types, even though they all share basic personality features typical of the breed.

Individual dachshunds may exhibit different temperaments from those described below, independent of coat type. A dachshund's personality is also significantly influenced by factors including genetics, socialization, and upbringing. Spending time with many dachshunds will help you choose which personality best fits your tastes and way of life if you're thinking about getting one.

Smooth Haired Dachshund

This is the most common Dachshund coat. Their smooth fur increases their ability to chase their prey quickly, since they were designed as hunting dogs.

• Intelligent • Loyal • Playful • Territorial • Chasers • Greedy • Courageous • Cuddly • Loving • Highly devoted to their owners

Long-Haired Dachshund

They are able to stay warm for longer periods of time while hunting outdoors in cold weather because of their thicker coats, which help them retain their body heat.

• Loyal but not always with the same person. • Slightly less noisy than Wire and Smooth-Haired • Stubborn • Are not very cuddly • Sweet-natured • Most burrow • Affectionate • Calm • Gentle • Affectionate

Wire-Haired Dachshund

Their hair gives them a level of protection from thorns and other vegetation when they are out hunting for prey.

• Noisier • Slightly more aggressive • Feisty • Prone to feeling anxious • Protective • Aggressive • Jealous • The funniest • Not quite as cuddly as Smooth-Haired • Mischievous
